# Mathematical Lens

Climate monitoring should be evaluated as a long-duration record-stewardship system, not only as an observation feed.

## Climate anomaly

\[
A_t = X_t - \bar{X}_{B}
\]

A climate anomaly compares a value at time \(t\) against a defined baseline period.

## Simple trend model

\[
X_t = \alpha + \beta t + \varepsilon_t
\]

A simple trend model estimates systematic change through time. Operational climate analysis should account for autocorrelation, missingness, variability, uncertainty, and physical context.

## Record continuity

\[
C_{\mathrm{record}} =
\frac{N_{\mathrm{valid\ time\ steps}}}{N_{\mathrm{expected\ time\ steps}}}
\]

Record continuity measures the share of expected time steps with valid observations or accepted record values.

## Geographic coverage

\[
G_{\mathrm{coverage}} =
\frac{A_{\mathrm{observed}}}{A_{\mathrm{target}}}
\]

Geographic coverage compares the observed area or target domain against the intended monitoring domain.

## Climate-record evidence quality

\[
Q_{\mathrm{climate\ record}} =
w_1C_r +
w_2M_c +
w_3K_c +
w_4H_r +
w_5S_c +
w_6V_c +
w_7U_c +
w_8G_r
\]

Climate-record evidence quality depends on continuity, metadata completeness, calibration quality, homogenization readiness, spatial coverage, version control, uncertainty communication, and governance readiness.
