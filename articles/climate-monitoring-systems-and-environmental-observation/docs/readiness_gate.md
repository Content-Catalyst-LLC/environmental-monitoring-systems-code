# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before a climate monitoring system is used for trend detection, climate assessment, reanalysis, model evaluation, adaptation planning, infrastructure standards, public reporting, environmental accountability, or climate services, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Checks

1. Climate variable, domain, geography, baseline, temporal scale, decision use, and evidence standard are defined.
2. Stations, satellites, ocean platforms, cryosphere sites, flux towers, or specialty systems are documented.
3. Instrument, location, siting, method, algorithm, ownership, and processing histories are preserved.
4. Reference standards, sensor transitions, satellite intercalibration, and overlap periods are documented.
5. Observations are quality-controlled, versioned, and transformed into comparable climate records.
6. Break points, non-climatic artifacts, missingness, and adjustments are documented.
7. Spatial, temporal, vertical, ocean, polar, and lower-capacity-region gaps are identified.
8. Record limitations, processing assumptions, coverage constraints, and valid-use caveats are exposed.
9. Stewardship, revision, access, citation, archive, equity, and accountability responsibilities are documented.
