# Orbital Evidence Framework

Satellite observation is a chain of evidence from orbital signal to Earth-system claim.

## Core layers

| Layer | Question | Evidence artifact |
|---|---|---|
| Mission and orbit | Does orbit, revisit, coverage, and mission purpose match the environmental question? | Mission profile |
| Instrument | What signal is measured and with what spectral, radiometric, or geometric constraints? | Instrument specification |
| Acquisition | Were observations captured, downlinked, and registered? | Acquisition log |
| Calibration and geolocation | Are measurements corrected and spatially meaningful? | Calibration and geolocation QA |
| Retrieval and product | How does signal become geophysical product? | Retrieval card and product registry |
| Continuity | Are products comparable across missions and algorithms? | Continuity plan |
| Integration | How are products combined with in situ observations and models? | Integration manifest |
| Decision support | Which claim, warning, report, or policy use does the product support? | Decision-use statement |
| Governance | Who stewards, validates, updates, and explains the product? | Product governance log |
