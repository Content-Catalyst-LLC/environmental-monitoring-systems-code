# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before soil monitoring is used for irrigation scheduling, drought assessment, nutrient management, salinity control, soil-health reporting, runoff-risk interpretation, food-system resilience planning, or public environmental claims, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Checks

1. Monitoring objective and decision use are defined.
2. Sensor placement represents soil zones and management zones.
3. Measurements align with root-zone depth and crop stage.
4. Calibration and maintenance records are current.
5. Telemetry completeness, stale data, and outages are visible.
6. Laboratory results support nutrient, pH, texture, carbon, and soil-health claims.
7. Remote products are validated against field observations before local use.
8. Thresholds and recommendations have a soil, crop, and management rationale.
9. Public claims include uncertainty, scale, and valid-use caveats.
