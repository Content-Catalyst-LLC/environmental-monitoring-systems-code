# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before a warning system is used for public alerting, evacuation, infrastructure shutdown, anticipatory action, or emergency coordination, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Readiness checks

1. Hazard signals are monitored with adequate coverage, redundancy, and freshness.
2. Forecast models, thresholds, and uncertainty practices are validated.
3. Warning authority and escalation procedures are documented.
4. Warning channels are redundant, accessible, and tested.
5. Warnings are plain-language, location-specific, and action-oriented.
6. Preparedness drills show that institutions and communities can act.
7. Last-mile audits identify language, disability, mobility, housing, and digital-access gaps.
8. Event evidence packages can reconstruct detection, decision, message, delivery, and response.
9. After-action review produces threshold, message, training, and governance improvements.
