# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before a land-use monitoring system is used for carbon accounting, biodiversity assessment, restoration claims, land-degradation reporting, urban planning, watershed management, climate reporting, compliance, or public communication, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Readiness checks

1. Monitoring purpose, geography, temporal window, class system, and evidence standard are defined.
2. Land-cover, land-use, condition, and change categories are documented and not conflated.
3. Imagery, sensor lineage, acquisition dates, cloud masks, and preprocessing steps are recorded.
4. Time-series methods distinguish persistent change from phenology, temporary disturbance, and classification noise.
5. Reference samples and validation data are independent, documented, and fit for purpose.
6. Accuracy, class-specific performance, change confidence, uncertainty, and area-estimation methods are reported.
7. Condition, fragmentation, degradation, intensification, or recovery are measured where broad classes are insufficient.
8. Public-facing claims are matched to validation strength and uncertainty.
9. Product revisions, disputed interpretations, and caveats are governed and versioned.
