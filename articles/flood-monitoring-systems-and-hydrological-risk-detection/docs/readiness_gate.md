# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before flood monitoring is used for warning, evacuation support, road closure, reservoir operations, emergency response, public dashboards, planning claims, or resilience assessment, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Checks

1. Flood type, geography, lead-time goal, protected population, and decision use are defined.
2. Stage, discharge, rainfall, soil wetness, snowmelt, reservoir, and other relevant drivers are covered.
3. Gauge and rainfall blind spots are identified, especially in flash-flood and ungauged-basin contexts.
4. Telemetry latency, missingness, stale data, and outage status are visible.
5. Gauge status, rating curves, rainfall products, and model inputs are quality-checked.
6. Forecast models, flash-flood guidance, forecast horizon, and uncertainty are documented.
7. Hydrological thresholds are linked to inundation, roads, structures, critical facilities, and exposed populations.
8. Warnings are authoritative, understandable, channel-diverse, and tied to action guidance.
9. Emergency response workflows, agency coordination, road-closure logic, and evacuation support are defined.
10. Equity, public caveats, threshold revisions, forecast errors, and post-event learning are governed.
