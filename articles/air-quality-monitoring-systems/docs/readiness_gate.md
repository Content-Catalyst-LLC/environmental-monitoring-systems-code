# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before an air quality monitoring system is used for public alerts, regulatory reporting, community exposure claims, environmental justice assessment, intervention evaluation, public-health guidance, or planning decisions, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Checks

1. Pollutant targets, geography, population, time scale, monitoring purpose, and decision use are defined.
2. Monitor locations, platform types, sensor density, and hotspot-coverage decisions are justified.
3. Reference comparisons, collocation studies, correction models, and recalibration routines are documented.
4. Data completeness, drift, humidity, cross-sensitivity, outliers, and sensor faults are handled.
5. Meteorology, source proximity, land use, and temporal patterns are integrated into interpretation.
6. Concentration data can be interpreted in relation to people, places, vulnerability, and microenvironments.
7. Dashboards, AQI outputs, alerts, maps, and reports are clear, accurate, and uncertainty-aware.
8. High-burden communities, near-source exposure zones, and under-monitored areas are represented.
9. Data stewardship, public access, intervention review, uncertainty, and corrective actions are documented.
