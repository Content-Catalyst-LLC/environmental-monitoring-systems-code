# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before smart water is used for treatment optimization, water-quality surveillance, leakage detection, AMI operations, wet-weather control, digital twin operation, automated control, public reporting, or resilience planning, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Checks

1. Service goal, water domain, decision use, and response expectation are defined.
2. Sensor coverage and blind spots are documented.
3. Telemetry latency, completeness, stale data, and outages are visible.
4. QA/QC rules handle drift, fouling, flatlines, missing data, and false values.
5. Alert thresholds and anomaly rules are validated and governed.
6. Models or digital twins have model cards, data feeds, calibration, and assumptions.
7. Alerts connect to response workflows, work orders, confirmation sampling, or operational action.
8. Automated or semi-automated control has fail-safes, override, and safety constraints.
9. Workforce capacity, cybersecurity, public accountability, privacy, and equity are addressed.
