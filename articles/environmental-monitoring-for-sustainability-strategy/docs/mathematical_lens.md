# Mathematical Lens

Environmental monitoring for sustainability strategy should evaluate indicators as governed evidence objects.

## Target gap

\[
G_{\mathrm{target}} = \frac{S_t - T}{B - T}
\]

Target gap compares current state \(S_t\) with target \(T\) relative to baseline \(B\).

## Threshold distance

\[
D_{\mathrm{threshold}} = \frac{\Theta - S_t}{\Theta}
\]

Threshold distance measures how close the current condition is to an ecological, regulatory, public-health, or system limit.

## Indicator integrity

\[
I_{\mathrm{integrity}} = w_1M + w_2V + w_3L + w_4U + w_5C
\]

Indicator integrity combines metadata completeness, validation, lineage, uncertainty treatment, and conceptual relevance.

## Feedback strength

\[
F_{\mathrm{feedback}} = \frac{N_{\mathrm{corrective\ actions}}}{N_{\mathrm{material\ findings}}}
\]

Feedback strength measures whether material findings lead to corrective action.

## Accountability

\[
A_{\mathrm{accountability}} = \frac{N_{\mathrm{publicly\ reviewable\ findings}}}{N_{\mathrm{material\ findings}}}
\]

Accountability can be approximated as the share of material findings available for public or external review.

## Rebound risk

\[
R_{\mathrm{rebound}} = \Delta E_{\mathrm{efficiency}} - \Delta P_{\mathrm{absolute}}
\]

Rebound risk appears when efficiency improves but absolute environmental pressure does not decline accordingly.
