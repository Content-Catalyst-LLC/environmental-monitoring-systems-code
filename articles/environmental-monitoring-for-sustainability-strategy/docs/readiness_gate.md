# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before a sustainability monitoring system is used for strategy, reporting, investment, governance, or accountability claims, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Readiness checks

1. The sustainability question is clearly defined.
2. Indicators are classified as state, pressure, response, outcome, resilience, or equity.
3. Each material indicator has a unit, source, baseline, target, threshold, method, uncertainty statement, and owner.
4. Targets are compared with relevant ecological, regulatory, public-health, or system thresholds.
5. Absolute pressure indicators are tracked alongside intensity and efficiency metrics.
6. Tradeoff registers identify burden shifting, rebound risk, and cross-system effects.
7. Material findings trigger assigned corrective actions.
8. Evidence is traceable through lineage and version history.
9. Public evidence packages make methods, limitations, and findings reviewable.
