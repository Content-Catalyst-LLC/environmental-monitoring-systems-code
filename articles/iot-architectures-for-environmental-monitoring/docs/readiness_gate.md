# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before an environmental IoT architecture is used for public communication, operational response, regulatory reporting, infrastructure monitoring, ecological assessment, sustainability strategy, or accountability, it should pass a readiness gate.

1. Monitoring purpose, domains, decision uses, and latency classes are defined.
2. Devices are identified, calibrated, located, powered, protected, and health-monitored.
3. Gateway functions, local rules, buffering, and failover behavior are documented.
4. Connectivity supports required latency, completeness, recoverability, and security.
5. Platform ingestion validates required schema and metadata.
6. Observed properties, units, locations, timestamps, calibration status, and quality flags are consistent.
7. Quality-control rules cover range, spike, flatline, drift, missingness, and suspect-data handling.
8. Alerts are actionable, prioritized, owned, suppress duplicate noise, and are reviewed.
9. Security and access governance are defined.
10. Maintenance, calibration, firmware, device replacement, and public caveats are governed.
