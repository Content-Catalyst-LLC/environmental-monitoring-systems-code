# Inference and Proxy Card

## Purpose

This placeholder card documents how a remote-sensing signal becomes an environmental product.

## Product family

Remote sensing systems in environmental monitoring.

## Evidence status

A remote-sensing product should not be described as direct environmental reality unless the product explicitly represents measured signal. Most products transform reflectance, radiance, thermal emission, backscatter, point clouds, or related signals into indices, retrievals, classifications, or inferred indicators.

## Required documentation

- source platform
- sensor
- sensing mode
- spectral or measurement characteristics
- preprocessing steps
- correction methods
- quality masks
- inference method
- index, retrieval, or classification definition
- validation datasets
- uncertainty method
- known failure regimes
- valid-use statement
- product version
- decision-use limits

## Known risks

- proxy overconfidence
- visual overconfidence
- weak field validation
- classification drift
- sensor-transition discontinuity
- cloud or atmosphere bias
- scale mismatch
- public overinterpretation
