# Remote-Sensing Evidence Framework

Remote sensing is a chain of evidence from measured signal to environmental claim.

## Core layers

| Layer | Question | Evidence artifact |
|---|---|---|
| Observation objective | What environmental process, variable, scale, and decision use are being monitored? | Objective manifest |
| Platform | What vantage point, revisit, coverage, and operational constraints apply? | Platform inventory |
| Sensor | What signal is measured, and with what spectral, thermal, radar, lidar, or structural capability? | Sensor specification |
| Preprocessing | How are measurements calibrated, corrected, masked, filtered, and geolocated? | Preprocessing manifest |
| Derivation | How does signal become index, classification, retrieval, anomaly, or change product? | Inference and proxy card |
| Validation | How is product performance tested with reference or field data? | Validation protocol |
| Time series | How are repeated observations harmonized across seasons, sensors, and versions? | Time-series manifest |
| Decision support | Which monitoring, planning, response, or public evidence task does the product support? | Decision-support matrix |
| Governance | Who stewards, reviews, updates, and explains the product? | Product governance log |
