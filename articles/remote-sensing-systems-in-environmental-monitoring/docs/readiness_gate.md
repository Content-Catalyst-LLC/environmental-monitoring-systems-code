# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before a remote-sensing product is used for public communication, hazard response, planning, ecological assessment, regulatory review, sustainability reporting, or accountability, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Readiness checks

1. Platform, sensor, resolution, revisit, and coverage match the environmental question.
2. Product metadata include platform, sensor, sensing mode, product level, variable, unit, grid, cadence, and version.
3. Calibration, correction, geolocation, masking, and filtering steps are documented.
4. Signal-to-product inference method is documented.
5. Validation evidence exists for relevant geography, season, class, land cover, and environmental regime.
6. Uncertainty, confidence, quality flags, caveats, and valid-use limits are visible.
7. Time-series continuity is documented where change detection or trend interpretation matters.
8. Field validation and model integration are documented where claims require more than remote evidence alone.
9. Decision-use and user roles are explicit.
10. Product governance and public caveat policy are defined.
