# Proxy, Uncertainty, and Validation Notes

Remote-sensing products can be visually powerful while remaining inferentially complex.

## Proxy questions

- Is the product measured, corrected, indexed, retrieved, classified, modeled, or scenario-based?
- What physical signal is being measured?
- What environmental state is inferred from that signal?
- What assumptions connect the signal to the claim?
- Where is the product valid?
- Where does the inference weaken?

## Uncertainty questions

- Is uncertainty provided as a layer, score, confidence interval, quality flag, caveat, or validation statistic?
- Is uncertainty visible to downstream users?
- Are indices, classifications, retrievals, and inferred outputs labeled distinctly?
- Is product confidence known across regions, seasons, classes, and environmental conditions?

## Validation questions

- What reference data were used?
- Are reference data independent?
- Are validation data spatially and temporally aligned?
- Are class-specific accuracies reported?
- Are field validation gaps documented?
- Are public-facing products clear about limits?
