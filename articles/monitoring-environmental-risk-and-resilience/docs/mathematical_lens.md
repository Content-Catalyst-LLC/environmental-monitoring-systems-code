# Mathematical Lens

Environmental risk and resilience monitoring should distinguish hazard, exposure, vulnerability, capacity, thresholds, recovery, and adaptive learning.

## Risk

\[
R_{\mathrm{risk}} = H \times E \times V \times (1 - C)
\]

Risk increases with hazard, exposure, and vulnerability, and decreases as coping or adaptive capacity improves.

## Threshold proximity

\[
P_{\mathrm{threshold}} = \frac{X_t}{X_{\mathrm{critical}}}
\]

Threshold proximity compares current stress or system state to a critical threshold.

## Recovery ratio

\[
R_{\mathrm{recovery}}(t) = \frac{F(t)}{F_{\mathrm{target}}}
\]

Recovery ratio measures restored function over time relative to a target function.

## Resilience score

\[
S_{\mathrm{resilience}} = w_1C + w_2R_d + w_3L + w_4G + w_5E_c - w_6V
\]

Resilience combines capacity, redundancy, learning, governance, ecological condition, and vulnerability.

## Change in risk

\[
\Delta R_{\mathrm{risk}} = R_{\mathrm{risk,post}} - R_{\mathrm{risk,pre}}
\]

Post-event monitoring should determine whether recovery and adaptation reduce or reproduce future risk.
