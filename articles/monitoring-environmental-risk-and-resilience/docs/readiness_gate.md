# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before a risk and resilience monitoring system is used for planning, public communication, early warning, funding, or recovery governance, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Readiness checks

1. Hazard signals are valid, timely, and spatially appropriate.
2. Exposure layers include people, assets, services, ecosystems, and infrastructure.
3. Vulnerability indicators are justified and disaggregated.
4. Capacity indicators represent preparedness, redundancy, governance, and adaptive capacity.
5. Thresholds include uncertainty bands, trigger rationale, and escalation rules.
6. Recovery tracking continues beyond immediate damage assessment.
7. Equity audits identify unequal exposure, warning, response, and recovery.
8. Governance responsibilities and data stewardship are explicit.
