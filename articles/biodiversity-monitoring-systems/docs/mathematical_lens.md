# Mathematical Lens

Biodiversity monitoring should be evaluated as an evidence system, not only as a collection of species records.

## Detection-adjusted occupancy

\[
\psi_{\mathrm{adjusted}} = \frac{\psi_{\mathrm{observed}}}{p_{\mathrm{detect}}}
\]

Observed occupancy should be interpreted in relation to imperfect detection. A non-detection is not necessarily absence.

## Taxonomic coverage

\[
C_{\mathrm{taxa}} = \frac{N_{\mathrm{taxa\ monitored}}}{N_{\mathrm{taxa\ target}}}
\]

Taxonomic coverage measures how much of the target biological scope is meaningfully represented.

## Spatial representativeness

\[
R_{\mathrm{spatial}} = 1 - \frac{G_{\mathrm{underobserved}}}{G_{\mathrm{target}}}
\]

Spatial representativeness measures whether target habitats, regions, gradients, or community-managed places are observed.

## Method diversity

\[
D_{\mathrm{method}} = \frac{N_{\mathrm{methods\ used}}}{N_{\mathrm{methods\ needed}}}
\]

Method diversity matters because field, acoustic, camera, eDNA, remote-sensing, and community-based methods observe different parts of biodiversity.

## Biodiversity evidence quality

\[
Q_{\mathrm{biodiversity\ evidence}} =
w_1C_{\mathrm{taxa}} +
w_2R_{\mathrm{spatial}} +
w_3D_{\mathrm{method}} +
w_4A_{\mathrm{detectability}} +
w_5Q_{\mathrm{observation}} +
w_6I_{\mathrm{indicator}} +
w_7H_{\mathrm{habitat}} +
w_8U_{\mathrm{uncertainty}} +
w_9G_{\mathrm{governance}}
\]

Biodiversity evidence quality depends on taxonomic coverage, spatial representativeness, method diversity, detectability adjustment, observation quality, indicator readiness, habitat linkage, uncertainty communication, and governance readiness.
