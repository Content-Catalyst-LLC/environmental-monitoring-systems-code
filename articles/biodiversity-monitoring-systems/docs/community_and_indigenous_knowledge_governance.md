# Community-Based and Indigenous Knowledge Governance

Biodiversity monitoring becomes stronger when it expands both empirical coverage and epistemic legitimacy. Community-based, Indigenous, local, and citizen-science monitoring can observe ecological change across places, seasons, and lifeways that formal programs often miss.

## Governance principles

- prior consent and clear purpose
- respect for knowledge ownership
- transparent data-use agreements
- locally meaningful ecological categories
- right to restrict sensitive locations or culturally significant knowledge
- avoidance of extractive data collection
- shared interpretation where appropriate
- benefit, capacity, and stewardship reciprocity
- clear public caveats when local observations are integrated into standardized indicators

## Technical implication

Community observations should not be reduced to raw data points without preserving interpretive context, provenance, ownership, and valid-use limits.
