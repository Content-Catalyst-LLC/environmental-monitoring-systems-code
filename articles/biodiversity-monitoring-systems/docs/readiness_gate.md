# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before a biodiversity monitoring system is used for conservation claims, restoration reporting, invasive-species response, biodiversity finance, GBF reporting, protected-area evaluation, national biodiversity strategy, or public communication, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Readiness checks

1. Monitoring purpose, target taxa, ecosystem context, geography, temporal window, and reporting use are defined.
2. Species occurrence, abundance, population trend, distribution, community composition, habitat, and ecosystem context are distinguished.
3. Observation methods are documented with effort, timing, spatial coverage, and method-specific limits.
4. Detectability, non-detection, effort, observer bias, sensor placement, and taxonomic bias are addressed.
5. Indicators and EBVs are linked to underlying observations and not treated as raw biodiversity itself.
6. Habitat and ecosystem context are included where species observations alone are insufficient.
7. Community-based, Indigenous, local, and citizen-science knowledge are governed with consent, ownership, and interpretive respect.
8. Uncertainty and representativeness limits are publicly visible.
9. Conservation, restoration, finance, or policy claims are matched to evidence quality.
