# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before a river or watershed monitoring network is used for flood forecasting, drought planning, nutrient-load reduction, ecological assessment, restoration targeting, water allocation, transboundary negotiation, source-water protection, or public environmental claims, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Checks

1. Basin boundary, decision use, target variables, agencies, and time horizon are defined.
2. Monitoring nodes are linked to tributaries, confluences, upstream areas, outlets, reservoirs, and managed infrastructure.
3. Headwaters, tributaries, main stems, outlets, groundwater connections, and high-pressure areas are represented.
4. Flow, quality, ecological, meteorological, groundwater, land-use, and infrastructure variables are sufficient for the intended claim.
5. Sampling and telemetry frequencies are adequate for trends, low-flow stress, storms, floods, or event loads.
6. Rating curves, sensor drift, sample QA/QC, missingness, and station health are managed.
7. Claims about loads, sources, trends, restoration effects, or basin exports have model cards and uncertainty statements.
8. Data can be discovered, shared, combined, and reused across agencies and jurisdictions.
9. Public accountability, caveats, cross-boundary coordination, funding, and revision responsibilities are defined.
