# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before an environmental sensor network is used for public communication, environmental reporting, hazard alerting, infrastructure monitoring, ecological assessment, water-quality review, air-quality monitoring, sustainability strategy, or regulatory support, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Readiness checks

1. Network purpose, environmental process, spatial scale, temporal scale, decision uses, and evidence standard are defined.
2. Topology captures relevant gradients, hotspots, reference sites, vulnerable places, and known blind spots.
3. Nodes are identified, calibrated, maintained, located, powered, and health-monitored.
4. Telemetry supports required completeness, latency, retry behavior, and delayed-record handling.
5. Metadata preserves node identity, location, observed property, unit, time, calibration status, quality flag, and provenance.
6. QA/QC rules cover range, spike, flatline, drift, missingness, stale calibration, and cross-node consistency.
7. Interoperability supports discovery, comparison, reuse, integration, and audit.
8. Alerts have thresholds, severity, owners, suppression rules, escalation pathways, and after-action review.
9. Coverage and equity audits identify uneven visibility across communities, ecosystems, or risk zones.
10. Governance defines stewardship, maintenance, public caveats, access, review cadence, and corrective learning.
