# Deployment Readiness Gate

Before a water quality monitoring system is used for public-health protection, aquatic-life assessment, regulatory compliance, recreational advisories, treatment operations, restoration evaluation, source-water protection, wastewater review, public reporting, or sustainability claims, it should pass a readiness gate.

## Checks

1. Waterbody type, use, risk question, decision context, and parameter rationale are defined.
2. Sites, depths, locations, hydrological settings, and exposure contexts are representative of the intended claim.
3. Sensors, grab samples, composite samples, autosamplers, biological protocols, or laboratory methods are fit for purpose.
4. Calibration, drift, fouling, blanks, duplicates, detection limits, chain of custody, and review flags are handled.
5. Flow, stage, runoff, event timing, dilution, residence time, or load context is recorded where needed.
6. Analytical specificity, detection limits, preservation, chain of custody, and turnaround are appropriate.
7. Standards, criteria, advisories, operational thresholds, or ecological benchmarks are defined.
8. Data can be combined with hydrology, site metadata, laboratory records, public reporting, and governance systems.
9. Caveats, public evidence, capacity limits, equity, access, and revision responsibilities are documented.
