# Mathematical Lens

Water quality monitoring should be evaluated as an aquatic evidence system, not only as a parameter list.

## Instantaneous load

\[
L(t) = C(t)Q(t)
\]

Instantaneous load is concentration multiplied by discharge.

## Event load

\[
L_{\mathrm{event}} = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} C(t)Q(t)\,dt
\]

Event load estimates total transported mass during a storm, pulse, treatment event, flood, or management-relevant period.

## Exceedance ratio

\[
R_{\mathrm{exceedance}} = \frac{N_{\mathrm{exceedances}}}{N_{\mathrm{valid\ observations}}}
\]

Exceedance ratio summarizes how often valid observations exceed a threshold, criterion, advisory value, or operational limit.

## Simplified exposure

\[
E_{\mathrm{exposure}} = C \times T \times F
\]

Exposure depends on concentration, duration, and frequency of contact. Real public-health and ecological assessments require parameter-specific methods.

## Water-quality evidence quality

\[
Q_{\mathrm{water\ quality\ evidence}} =
w_1S_m +
w_2A_v +
w_3H_c +
w_4Q_c +
w_5L_r +
w_6T_a +
w_7U_c +
w_8D_i +
w_9G_r
\]

Water-quality evidence quality depends on sampling representativeness, analytical validity, hydrological context, QA/QC completeness, load readiness, threshold alignment, uncertainty communication, data interoperability, and governance readiness.
